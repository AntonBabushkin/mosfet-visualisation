Operation Overview

This simulator visualizes the n-channel MOSFET behavior under different gate–source (VGS) and drain–source (VDS) voltages. Adjust the sliders to explore how the channel forms and how the device current responds.

Modes of Operation

  • Cutoff region: When VGS < VTH, the transistor is off — no conduction channel is formed, and drain current ≈ 0.
  • Linear (Ohmic) region: When VGS > VTH and VDS < VGS − VTH, the device acts as a voltage-controlled resistor.
  • Saturation region: When VDS ≥ VGS − VTH, the channel pinches off near the drain, and current is limited by the overdrive voltage.

Controls

  • VGS — sets gate voltage and controls channel formation.
  • VDS — sets drain voltage, controlling the potential drop along the channel.
  • Length / Width toggles — change device geometry. A shorter channel increases current and channel-length modulation, while a wider device increases total current proportionally.

Model Equations

The simulator uses the classical square-law MOSFET model with a simple channel-length modulation term.

Cutoff region (VGS < VTH):

$$ I_D = 0 $$

Linear (Ohmic) region (VDS < VGS − VTH):

$$ I_D = K \left[ (V_{GS} - V_{TH})V_{DS} - \tfrac{1}{2}V_{DS}^2 \right] $$

Saturation region (VDS ≥ VGS − VTH):

$$ I_D = \tfrac{1}{2}K(V_{GS} - V_{TH})^2\bigl(1 + \lambda(V_{DS} - V_{DS,sat})\bigr) $$

$$ V_{DS,sat} = V_{GS} - V_{TH} $$

The process-dependent transconductance parameter (sometimes called the gain factor) \( K \) is defined as:

$$ K = \tfrac{1}{2}\mu_n C_{ox}\frac{W}{L} $$

Here, \( \mu_n \) is the electron mobility, \( C_{ox} \) is the gate-oxide capacitance per unit area, \( W \) and \( L \) are the channel width and length, \( \lambda \) is the channel-length modulation coefficient, and \( V_{TH} \) is the threshold voltage.

The small-signal transconductance is related to \( K \) by \( g_m = K(V_{GS} - V_{TH}) \), showing that \( g_m \) increases linearly with overdrive voltage.
